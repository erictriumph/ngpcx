About CheckMyARM

How we classify apps

CheckMyARM maintains an evolving, evidence-based compatibility database. Applications without verified compatibility data are marked Unclassified. Most conventional x86/x64 Windows applications are expected to run under emulation until confirmed native or unsupported.

Why "CheckMyARM" runs on something called NGPCX

CheckMyARM runs on NGPCX (Next-Gen PC eXplorer) — we just like the cute name better.

Privacy

The scanner talks to your browser over localhost:7878 — that connection never leaves your machine. All real network traffic (submitting scan results, checking compatibility data) goes over HTTPS on port 443 to the CheckMyARM server, the same as any normal website.

Support

CheckMyARM is free and we intend to keep it that way — with no ads. If it saved you some research, consider buying us a coffee or using our affiliate links when you buy your next laptop.
